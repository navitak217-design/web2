ACTIVE AND PASSIVE VOICE

What is Active Voice?

A sentence is in active voice when the subject does the action.
Examples:
Ali writes a letter.
She cooks food.
They play cricket.

What is Passive Voice?

A sentence is in passive voice when the action is done to the subject.
Examples:
A letter is written by Ali.
Food is cooked by her.
Cricket is played by them.

Basic Rule (Active â†’ Passive)

Object of the active sentence becomes the subject.
Use helping verb according to tense.
Use 3rd form of verb (past participle).
Use by before the doer (subject).

Passive Voice Formula

Subject + helping verb + past participle + by + object

Active to Passive (Common Tenses)

Simple Present

Active: She writes a letter.
Passive: A letter is written by her.

Present Continuous

Active: He is cleaning the room. Passive: The room is being cleaned by him.

Simple Past

Active: Ali wrote a letter.
Passive: A letter was written by Ali.

Simple Future

Active: They will complete the work.
Passive: The work will be completed by them.

When NOT to use Passive Voice

When subject is unknown or unimportant In informal or spoken English
Example:
I did my homework. (Better than passive)
