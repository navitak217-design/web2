Tenses

You can divide Tenses into 3 main types and explain each with definition, structure, and examples on your grammar website.

1. Present Tense

a) Simple Present Tense

Used for daily routines, habits, and general facts. Structure: Subject + base verb (+ s/es)
Example:
I play cricket.
She reads a book.

b) Present Continuous Tense

Used for actions happening now. Structure: Subject + is/am/are + verb-ing.
Example:
I am studying English.
They are watching TV.

c) Present Perfect Tense

Used for actions completed recently or with result in present. Structure: Subject + has/have + past participle.
Example:
I have finished my homework.
She has gone to school.

d) Present Perfect Continuous Tense

Used for actions that started in the past and are still continuing. Structure: Subject + has/have been + verb-ing
Example:
I have been learning English for two years.
He has been working here since 2022.

2. Past Tense

a) Simple Past Tense

Used for actions completed in the past. Structure: Subject + past form of verb
Example:
I visited Lahore yesterday.
She wrote a letter.

b) Past Continuous Tense

Used for actions happening at a specific time in the past. Structure: Subject + was/were + verb-ing
Example:
I was reading a book.
They were playing football.

c) Past Perfect Tense

Used for an action completed before another past action. Structure: Subject + had + past participle
Example:
I had finished my work before sunset.
She had left when I arrived.

d) Past Perfect Continuous Tense

Used to show duration of an action before a past point. Structure: Subject + had been + verb-ing
Example:
I had been waiting for an hour.
They had been studying all night.

3. Future Tense

a) Simple Future Tense

Used for actions that will happen in the future. Structure: Subject + will + base verb.
Example:
I will help you.
She will call you tomorrow.

b) Future Continuous Tense

Used for actions that will be in progress at a future time. Structure: Subject + will be + verb-ing
Example:
I will be traveling tomorrow.
They will be working at night.

c) Future Perfect Tense

Used for actions that will be completed before a future time. Structure: Subject + will have + past participle
Example:
I will have completed my course.
She will have reached home by 8 pm.

d) Future Perfect Continuous Tense

Used to show duration of an action up to a future point. Structure: Subject + will have been + verb-ing
Example:
I will have been studying for three hours.
He will have been working here for five years.
